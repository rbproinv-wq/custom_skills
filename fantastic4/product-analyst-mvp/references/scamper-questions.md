# SCAMPER Questions for Product Ideation

SCAMPER is a creative brainstorming framework that helps refine and expand product ideas. Use these questions **during Step 2 (Apply a Product Discovery Framework)** to challenge assumptions and uncover new possibilities. Each letter prompts a different type of thinking.

---

## S – Substitute
*What can be replaced? Who or what else could fill the role?*

- What materials, components, or resources could be substituted?
- What if we replace human effort with automation? Or vice versa?
- Could we substitute the platform (e.g., WhatsApp → Telegram, web → mobile app)?
- What if we replace the payment model (subscription → pay-per-use)?
- Can we substitute a complex rule with a simpler heuristic?

**Example for clinic scheduling:**  
*Substitute receptionist with AI chatbot for after-hours booking.*

---

## C – Combine
*What can be merged? What features, roles, or processes can work together?*

- Can we combine two features into one to reduce friction?
- What if we merge the product with another tool the user already uses?
- Can we combine roles (e.g., “administrator + analyst”)? 
- What about combining multiple data sources into a single view?
- Could we offer a bundled service (product + support + training)?

**Example for clinic scheduling:**  
*Combine booking with reminder and patient intake form.*

---

## A – Adapt
*What can we copy, borrow, or adjust from other domains?*

- What similar products/solutions exist in a different industry? Can we adapt them?
- What features from a competitor could we adapt to our context?
- Can we adjust the user interface to match familiar patterns (e.g., calendar picker)?
- What existing API or service can we adapt instead of building from scratch?

**Example for clinic scheduling:**  
*Adapt ride-hailing app’s real-time availability model to medical appointments.*

---

## M – Modify/Magnify/Minify
*What can be enlarged, reduced, or altered?*

- **Magnify:** What if we increase speed, capacity, or frequency? Add more features?
- **Minify:** What if we reduce scope, complexity, or number of steps?
- **Modify:** Change the sequence, shape, color, or tone?
- Can we make it louder (more notifications) or quieter (less intrusive)?
- What if we stretch the timeframe (24h → 7 days for reminders)?

**Example for clinic scheduling:**  
*Reduce booking steps from 5 clicks to 2 messages.*

---

## P – Put to another use
*What other problems could this solution solve?*

- Can the product be used by a different customer segment?
- Is there a secondary market for the same functionality?
- Could this become a platform for other services?
- What if we offer the internal tool as a white-label product?

**Example for clinic scheduling:**  
*Use the same chatbot for rescheduling, canceling, and prescription refill requests.*

---

## E – Eliminate
*What can be removed entirely without breaking value?*

- Which features or steps cause the most friction? Can we remove them?
- What if we eliminate the need for passwords, onboarding, or manual data entry?
- Can we remove a whole persona (e.g., no admin needed for basic use)?
- What happens if we remove notifications, reports, or dashboards?
- What’s the absolute minimum that still solves the core problem?

**Example for clinic scheduling:**  
*Eliminate the need for phone calls entirely; AI handles everything except emergencies.*

---

## R – Reverse/Rearrange
*What if we do the opposite? Or change the order?*

- What if the process runs backwards (e.g., patient proposes times → clinic approves)?
- Can we swap who initiates the interaction (user → system)?
- Reverse the priority: what feature is least important? Make it most important.
- Rearrange the flow: confirmation before data collection?
- What if we charge after success instead of upfront?

**Example for clinic scheduling:**  
*Reverse: clinic sends available slots first, patient chooses one (instead of patient asking for times).*

---

## How to Use SCAMPER in This Skill

1. **Internal reasoning:** After understanding the user’s idea, run through SCAMPER questions silently to identify refinements or hidden opportunities.
2. **Output impact:** The insights from SCAMPER should influence the **Proposed Solution**, **MVP Features**, and **Future Work** sections of the PRD.
3. **User interaction (optional):** If the user is open to brainstorming, you can ask 1–2 SCAMPER questions (e.g., “Have you considered eliminating X?”).
4. **Documentation:** In the “Methodology used” section, you may mention “SCAMPER was applied to challenge assumptions and refine scope.”

> This reference is for the analyst agent’s internal use. Do not output SCAMPER questions in the final PRD unless specifically requested by the user.
