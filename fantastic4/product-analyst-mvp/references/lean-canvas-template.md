## Instructions for the Analyst

1. **Problem** – List the top 1–3 pains the customer experiences today. Be specific (e.g., "Waiting 2 days for a response" not "Bad service").
2. **Solution** – Map each problem to a high-level feature. Do not describe technical implementation.
3. **Unique Value Proposition** – A single sentence that differentiates your product from alternatives.
4. **Key Metrics** – Leading indicators that predict success (e.g., "Daily active users", "Time to book appointment").
5. **Unfair Advantage** – Something hard to replicate: exclusive data, domain expertise, network effects, etc.
6. **Channels** – How users discover and acquire the product (e.g., "WhatsApp API", "Referral program", "App store").
7. **Customer Segments** – Distinct groups with different needs. Include early adopters.
8. **Cost Structure** – Estimate fixed (setup) and variable (per use) costs. Use ranges if unknown.
9. **Revenue Streams** – If applicable, how the product generates value (freemium, subscription, transactional).
